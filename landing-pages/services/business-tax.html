Business Tax Frequently Asked Questions

Should my business be an LLC, S-Corp, or something else?

+

The best business structure depends on your specific situation, including your income level, business type, and growth plans. We analyze your business and provide recommendations that minimize taxes while optimizing liability protection. An S-Corp election can save 15% or more on self-employment taxes for profitable businesses. We'll help you make the right choice.

How often should I pay quarterly taxes?

+

Quarterly estimated tax payments are due four times per year: April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15. We calculate your quarterly obligations and remind you of payment dates so you stay compliant and avoid penalties. Our estimates are based on actual income projections for accuracy.

What business expenses can I deduct?

+

Legitimate business expenses are generally deductible, including office supplies, equipment, vehicle expenses, professional services, insurance, utilities, rent, and more. The key is that expenses must be ordinary, necessary, and directly related to your business. We help you properly document and categorize all deductible expenses to maximize your tax benefits while staying IRS compliant.

What records do I need to keep?

+

The IRS typically requires you to keep business records for at least three years, and sometimes longer for specific transactions. Essential records include income documentation, expense receipts, bank statements, invoices, payroll records, and asset depreciation schedules. Organized records make tax preparation easier and faster, saving you time and money during tax season.

Do I need to file sales tax returns?

+

If you sell products or taxable services in Illinois, you likely need to collect and file sales tax. The requirement depends on your business type and sales volume. We help you understand your sales tax obligations and ensure proper compliance with state and local requirements to avoid penalties.
