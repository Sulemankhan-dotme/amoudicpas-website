Frequently Asked Questions for Restaurant Owners

How should I handle tip reporting for my employees?

+

The IRS requires employees to report all tips of $20 or more per month. As an employer, you must collect tip reports from employees, include reported tips in payroll for tax withholding, and file Form 8027 if you operate a large food or beverage establishment. Our team handles all of this for you, ensuring full compliance while maximizing your FICA tip credit.

What tax deductions can restaurant owners claim?

+

Restaurant owners can deduct a wide range of expenses including food and beverage costs, employee wages and benefits, rent and utilities, kitchen equipment (often using Section 179 accelerated depreciation), marketing and advertising, insurance, licenses and permits, and even food donations to qualifying organizations. We ensure every eligible deduction is captured to minimize your tax liability.

Do I need a separate CPA who understands restaurants?

+

Restaurants have unique accounting challenges that general CPAs may overlook, including tip credit calculations, complex sales tax rules for different service types, high-volume payroll with varied employee classifications, and tight margin management. A CPA with restaurant experience understands these nuances and can save you significant money while keeping you out of trouble with the IRS and Illinois Department of Revenue.

How can you help me manage my food costs better?

+

We provide detailed food cost analysis by tracking your cost of goods sold as a percentage of revenue, comparing it to industry benchmarks, and identifying trends over time. We help you set up inventory tracking systems, analyze vendor pricing, evaluate menu item profitability, and develop strategies to reduce waste. Most restaurant clients see measurable improvement in their food cost percentages within the first few months.
